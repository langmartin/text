Meta Lessons

  • Me: we end making these design tradeoffs in applications
  • The market decides, not always wisely

Terms

  • Physical data independence: the ability to change unindexed data storage (like order of records) and run programs written for the old data storage layer

Hierarchical

  • Physical and logical data independence are highly desirable (but impossible)
  • Tree structured data models are very restrictive
  • It is a challenge to provide sophisticated logical reorganizations of tree structured data
  • A record-at-a-time user interface forces the programmer to do manual query optimization, and this is often hard.

Network

  • Networks are more flexible than hierarchies but more complex
  • Loading and recovering networks is more complex than hierarchies

Relational

Lessons

  1. Set-a-time languages are good, regardless of the data model, since they offer much improved physical data independence.
  2. Logical data independence is easier with a simple data model than with a complex one.
  3. Technical debates are usually settled by the elephants of the marketplace, and often for reasons that have little to do with the technology.
  4. Query optimizers can beat all but the best record-at-a-time DBMS application programmers.

Lessons

  1. Functional dependencies are too difficult for mere mortals to understand. Another reason for KISS
